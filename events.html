    Argus FMB Africa Fertilizer 2015


    Argus FMB Africa Fertilizer Conference is an annual event held in Africa. The conference provides you with the opportunity to meet representatives from the whole supply chain - from leading producers to major buyers and distributors, and all categories of essential service providers. The conference attracts over 450 attendees from 70 companies and over 60 countries. Each year we acquire high profile speakers from leading fertilizer production facilities, government members and NGO’s, providing discussions on the latest market issues to help develop the African agriculture and fertilizer sectors. The 2015 brings H.E. Tefera Derbew, the Minister of Agriculture for Ethiopia updating the audience on the development strategies for 2015, Khalid Bomba, CEO at the Agricultural Transformation Agency will discuss the need for transforming agriculture in Africa, Bashir Jama, the Soil Health Programme Director at AGRA will be covering the importance of complete fertilization of smallholder Aagriculture in Africa and Jean Bakole the Regional Director of Ethiopia from the United Nations Industrial Development Organization discusses available mineral resources in Africa. What you'll get at Africa’s only dedicated fertilizer conference:
    • 400+ attendees from across the fertilizer supply chain
    • The chance to meet fertilizer producers and traders from over 20+ African countries
    • 20+ hours of networking
    • Opening cocktail reception and gala dinner - exclusive to registered delegates
    • 20+ expert speakers
    • 3 days of discussions with senior decision makers
    Join the 6th Argus FMB Africa Fertilizer conference and discuss the future of Africa’s agriculture with the most influential players in the market. The 2015 event will be held on 18-20 February in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. For more information about Argus or attending the Argus FMB Africa Fertilizer conference:

    Argus Media


    Argus is an independent media organisation with over 700 full time staff. It is headquartered in London and has offices in each of the world’s principal commodity centres. Its main activities comprise publishing market reports containing price assessments, market commentary and news, and business intelligence reports that analyse market and industry trends. More than half of Argus employees are commodity journalists who specialise in reporting news and price information relating to physical energy and related commodity markets. They operate according to a rigorous Editorial Code of Conduct and a Compliance and Ethics Policy that align with best journalistic practice, including the avoidance of conflicts of interest. Argus is a leading provider of data on prices and fundamentals, news, analysis, consultancy services and conferences for the global crude, oil products, LPG, natural gas, electricity, coal, emissions, bioenergy, fertilizer, petrochemical, metals and transportation industries. Data provided by Argus are widely used for indexation of physical trade. Companies, governments and international agencies use Argus information for analysis and planning purposes. Argus has 20 offices globally, including London, Houston, Washington, New York, Calgary, Rio de Janeiro, Singapore, Dubai, Beijing, Tokyo, Sydney, Moscow, Astana, Riga and other key centres of the commodity industries. Argus was founded in 1970 and is a privately held UK-registered company.
